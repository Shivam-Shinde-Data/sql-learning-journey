# Day 24 – Interview Questions (Transactions & ACID)

## 1. What is a transaction in SQL?
A transaction is a group of operations executed as a single unit.

---

## 2. What is COMMIT?
It permanently saves changes made during a transaction.

---

## 3. What is ROLLBACK?
It undoes changes made in a transaction.

---

## 4. What is SAVEPOINT?
A point within a transaction to which you can roll back.

---

## 5. What does ACID stand for?
- Atomicity
- Consistency
- Isolation
- Durability

---

## 6. What is Atomicity?
All operations succeed or none are applied.

---

## 7. What is Consistency?
Database remains valid before and after transaction.

---

## 8. What is Isolation?
Transactions do not interfere with each other.

---

## 9. What is Durability?
Committed data is permanently stored.

---

## 10. What happens if a transaction fails?
It is rolled back to maintain consistency.

---

## 11. What are real-world examples of transactions?
Bank transfers, payments, booking systems.

---

## 12. Difference between COMMIT and ROLLBACK?

COMMIT:
- Saves changes permanently

ROLLBACK:
- Reverts changes
